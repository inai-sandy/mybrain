The promises

Six plain commitments.

No fine print games. Here's exactly how your data is treated.

🚫🤖

Never used to train AI

Your days, stories and notes are never training data — not for us, and not for the AI providers we use (their API terms forbid training on it).

🏠

Self-hosted

My Brain runs on your own server — not packaged up inside a big company's cloud where you're the product. Your data lives where you control it.

🗝️

Zero-knowledge Vault

Your secrets are locked with your own passphrase before they ever leave your device. Even the server can't read them — and they're never sent to the AI.

🧠

AI reads only to help you

When a feature needs AI, your words go to the model to write that one answer for you — and nothing more. They're not kept, profiled, or reused.

🗑️

Yours to delete

Delete anything, anytime — and it's actually gone. You're never locked in, and nothing is held hostage behind a paywall.

🙅

No ads, no selling

There are no ads and no data brokers. Your life isn't a profile to be sold. The only thing your data does is help you.

The honest part

Where AI fits in — said plainly.

My Brain uses AI to write your story, mentor you, and find things for you. To do that, the relevant words for that one task are sent to a language model, which writes the answer and is done. That's the same kind of trip your email or maps app makes — used to serve you in the moment, under API terms that forbid using it to train models.

What never makes that trip: anything in your Vault. Secrets are encrypted with your passphrase and stay unreadable to the server and the AI alike.

Most apps want your data.
This one just wants to help you.

Private by design isn't a feature we bolted on. It's the only way a second brain earns the honesty that makes it worth having.
